BAYESIAN STATISTICS

BAYES Factor: relative evidence for one model compared to the another.

You can never quantify the probability that a hypothesis is true; only the relative probability it is true, compared to another hypothesis.

BAYESIAN ESTIMATION: Instead testing prior and posterior we use prior to estimate the posterior.

A single credible interval around mean contains = 95% of the values you find most plausible, based on your prior belief and the data.

Absolute Evidence:

In Bayesian statistics, the probability of data under a specified model (D|P(H0) is a number that expressed what is sometimes referred to as the absolute evidence, and more formally referred to as a marginal likelihood.

Relative Evidence:

The relative evidence is calculated by dividing the marginal likelihood for one model by the marginal likelihood for another model. Bayes Factor

In Bayesian statistics, parameters are considered to be random variables, and the uncertainty or degree of belief with respect to the parameters is quantified by probability distributions.

Confidence Intervals

Whenever you estimate from sample the parameter for population, there is always some element of uncertainty.

Confidence intervals are a statement about the percentage of confidence intervals that contain the true parameter value.

Black lines represents the confidence intervals. The red lines do not represents the parameter value.

Most commonly we calculate the confidence intervals around mean or effect sizes.

For 95% CI:

CI becomes narrow as we increase the sample size.

Confidence intervals tell you something about what happens in the long run; Credible intervals quantify 95% of values you find most plausible.

Sample Size Justification

How to determine the sample size for new study?

Small Samples have Large Variations and more Type2 error rates and inaccurate estimates.

Large Sample size: more accurate estimates and higher power.

Approach:

  1. Planning for accuracy: Select Confidence Sample size based on width of the confidence interval.
  1. Design the study so that it will have specific statistical power. Select sample size based on probability of finding p < 0.05
  1. Planning for feasibility- Select sample size based on time, money, participants you have available.
